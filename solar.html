Planet Facts

  • The Sun is a star at the center of out solar system.

  • Its radiant energy sustains life on Earth, providing light, heat, and solar radiation.

  • Primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%) with trace elements.

  • The Sun is approximately 4.6 billion years old.

  • Nuclear fusion in its core converts hydrogen into helium, releasing immense energy.

  • The Sun's diameter is approximately 109 times that of Earth, with a volume over 1 million times Earth's.

  • Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.

  • It has a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers.

  • Mercury is the smallest "planet" in our solar system

  • Mercury has an extremely thin atmosphere.

  • It experiences extreme temperature variations.

  • The surface is heavily cratered like the Moon.

  • Venus is the second planet from the sun.

  • It's often called Earth's sister planet due to similar size and composition.

  • Venus has a thick atmosphere mainly of carbon dioxide, causing a runaway greenhouse effect.

  • Surface temperatures can reach 900Â°F, hot enough to melt lead.

  • It rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, called retrograde rotation.

  • Venus has no moons or natural satellites.

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun.

  • It has one natural satellite: the Moon.

  • Earth's atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen.

  • About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water.

  • It is the only known planet to support life.

  • Earth's magnetic field protects it from solar winds and cosmic rays.

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the sun, also known as the "Red Planet."

  • It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.

  • Mars has polar ice caps made of water and carbon dioxide.

  • The planet has the longest valley in the solar system, Valles Marineris.

  • It has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide.

  • Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.

  • Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system.

  • It has a giant red storm called the Great Red Spot.

  • Jupiter's moon Ganymede is the largest moon in the solar system, is even larger than the planet Mercury.

  • Its magnetic field is the strongest of all the planets.

  • Jupiter's composition is mainly hydrogen and helium.

  • It has a short day, completing a rotation in less than 10 hours.

  • Saturn is the second-largest planet in our solar system.

  • Saturn's rings are composed mostly of ice particles and dust.

  • Saturn's rings are divided into seven main groups, each with thousands of smaller rings.

  • Despite its large size, Saturn has the lowest density of all the planets in the solar system.

  • It has a short day, completing a rotation in 10.7 hours.

  • Saturn has the most moons in the solar system, with over 145 moons discovered.

  • Uranus is the seventh planet from the sun.

  • It has a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.

  • Uranus rotates on its side, making its axis almost parallel to its orbit.

  • It has faint rings and a diverse system of moons.

  • Uranus is an ice giant composed mostly of water, ammonia, and methane.

  • It takes about 84 Earth years to orbit the sun.

  • Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the sun.

  • It has a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.

  • Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching up to 1,500 mph.

  • It has a system of faint rings and a complex moon system.

  • Neptune is an ice giant with a composition similar to Uranus.

  • It was discovered in 1846 through mathematical predictions before it was seen.

  • Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt.

  • It was classified as the ninth planet until reclassification in 2006.

  • Pluto is smaller than Earth's moon.

  • Its surface is composed of nitrogen ice, with methane and water ice.

  • Pluto has a thin atmosphere containing nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide.

  • It has five known moons, the largest being Charon.
